A trip in the city of Irbid

18.05.2020

Irbid is a Jordanian city, and the center of Irbid governorate is in northern Jordan and its largest city. It is located 71 kilometers north of the capital, Amman, and about 20 km south of the Syrian-Jordanian border. It is the third largest city in the Kingdom after the Jordanian capital and Zarqa in terms of population, with a population of the city and its suburbs more than 530 thousand people, according to the population census for the year 2017, and the area of ??the city with its suburbs is estimated at about 30 square kilometers. The city is located in an average location for the rest of the province; It is located at 32 degrees north latitude and 35 degrees east longitude. The geographical nature of the city is distinguished by the plain nature, although the western regions of it are of a nature closer to the mountainous nature

Irbid villages The city of Irbid


embraces a large number of villages, estimated to number approximately 360 villages, and it is the most city that contains villages throughout the world, and these villages include Idoun, Zmmal, Habaka, Deir Yusef, the northern Mazar, Alal, Kafrioba, Barhah, and Hakama.

The city's heritage attractions


The city embraces a large number of heritage monuments that embody the ancient history of the city and the extent of its cultural depth, which are: the Museum of the Saraya. Irbid hill. Irbid Archeology Museum. Old Mamluk Mosque. The house of the Jordanian poet Arar. The old jewelery market. Sharif House.
